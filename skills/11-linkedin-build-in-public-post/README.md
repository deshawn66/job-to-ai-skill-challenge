# Skill 11: LinkedIn Build-In-Public Post

## What It Does

Turns real work into a clear LinkedIn post.

## Who It Helps

Operators who are building in public but do not want to sound like AI influencers.

## Inputs Needed

- What you built
- Why it mattered
- What surprised you
- What changed your thinking
- Link to the artifact
- Question for the audience

## Tools It May Interact With

- Notes
- GitHub
- Portfolio site
- LinkedIn

## Prompt

Write a LinkedIn post from this work. Use the structure: hook, problem, action, lesson, question. Keep the voice direct, specific, and human. Avoid jargon, hype, filler, emojis, and em dashes. Make the post about the lesson, not the tool.

## Sample Input

I tracked my workday, found 12 repeatable tasks, turned them into AI skills, published the repo, and built a portfolio site.

## Sample Output

I stopped asking AI vague questions and started giving it my actual job.

I wrote down how I spent one real workday.

Not the job description version.

The real version: follow-ups, reports, meetings, decisions, research, coaching, and the same questions showing up again.

Then I turned the repeatable parts into 12 AI skills.

Each skill has a job, an input, an output, a tool boundary, and a safety rule.

That changed how I think about AI.

The leverage is not in asking better one-off questions.

The leverage is in teaching the system how your work actually works.

What part of your job would you turn into a skill first?

## Safety Notes

- Do not reveal private business data.
- Do not exaggerate what was built.
- Do not publish links until they are ready.
