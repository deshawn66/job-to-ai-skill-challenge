# Skill 03: Meeting To Action Plan

## What It Does

Turns messy meeting notes into decisions, owners, open questions, and next steps.

## Who It Helps

Leaders who leave meetings with too many loose threads.

## Inputs Needed

- Meeting notes
- Transcript
- Agenda
- Attendee list
- Desired follow-up format

## Tools It May Interact With

- Calendar
- Notes
- Google Docs
- Team chat
- Project tracker

## Prompt

Read these meeting notes and create a clear action plan. Separate decisions, action items, owners, deadlines, open questions, risks, and follow-up messages. If an owner or deadline is missing, mark it as missing instead of guessing. Keep the tone direct and useful.

## Sample Input

We discussed the weekly sales report, a delayed partner handoff, a new training idea, and a dashboard issue. Maria will check the report. James will follow up with the partner. No date was set for the training.

## Sample Output

Decisions:

- Weekly sales report needs review before it is shared.
- Partner handoff needs direct follow-up.

Action items:

- Maria: check the weekly sales report. Deadline missing.
- James: follow up with the partner. Deadline missing.

Open questions:

- Who owns the training idea?
- When should the dashboard issue be fixed?

## Safety Notes

- Do not assign owners unless they are stated.
- Do not create fake deadlines.
- Do not include sensitive meeting details in public examples.
