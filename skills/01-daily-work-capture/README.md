# Skill 01: Daily Work Capture

## What It Does

Turns a messy workday into a clean task log.

## Who It Helps

Operators, founders, managers, and anyone whose real work is scattered across meetings, messages, documents, and decisions.

## Inputs Needed

- Calendar notes
- Meeting notes
- Message summaries
- Email highlights
- Tasks completed
- Decisions made
- Things that felt repetitive

## Tools It May Interact With

- Calendar
- Email
- Notes app
- Chat or messaging tools
- Task manager

## Prompt

You are helping me turn one real workday into an AI automation map. Read my rough notes and create a clean task log. Group the work by category: meetings, follow-ups, reports, decisions, research, handoffs, admin, and repeat questions. Mark each task as repeatable, judgment-heavy, research-heavy, admin-heavy, or relationship-heavy. Do not invent work I did not mention.

## Sample Input

I had a morning leadership meeting, reviewed pipeline issues, answered three follow-up questions from the sales team, checked a report, researched two AI tools, wrote a LinkedIn draft, and sent a handoff to operations.

## Sample Output

| Task | Category | Work Type | Automation Potential |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Morning leadership meeting | Meeting | Judgment-heavy | Medium |
| Reviewed pipeline issues | Report | Judgment-heavy | High |
| Answered sales follow-ups | Follow-up | Repeatable | High |
| Researched AI tools | Research | Research-heavy | Medium |
| Drafted LinkedIn post | Content | Repeatable | High |
| Sent operations handoff | Handoff | Admin-heavy | High |

## Safety Notes

- Do not include private names unless the user asks.
- Do not expose customer or employee-sensitive details.
- Do not turn rough notes into fake accomplishments.
