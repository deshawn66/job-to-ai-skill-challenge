# Skill 08: Playbook Builder

## What It Does

Turns a repeatable business motion into a simple playbook.

## Who It Helps

Operators, trainers, sales leaders, and managers.

## Inputs Needed

- Goal of the playbook
- Audience
- Current process
- Common mistakes
- Examples of good work
- Desired action after reading

## Tools It May Interact With

- Notes
- Training library
- Google Docs
- Internal wiki
- Sales enablement tools

## Prompt

Build a practical playbook from this process. Use plain English. Include the goal, who it is for, when to use it, step-by-step actions, examples, common mistakes, and a simple checklist. Make it usable by someone who has not heard me explain it live.

## Sample Input

Create a playbook for following up with past customers after a market update. Audience is sales professionals. Tone should be motivating and practical.

## Sample Output

Playbook title:

Past Customer Market Update Follow-Up

Goal:

Create useful conversations with past customers by giving them timely market context.

Steps:

1. Identify the past customer list.
2. Pick one simple market insight.
3. Send a short message.
4. Ask if they want a quick review.
5. Track the response.

Common mistakes:

- Making the message too long.
- Talking only about rates.
- Failing to ask a clear question.

## Safety Notes

- Do not make financial promises.
- Do not use regulated claims without review.
- Do not include private customer examples in public samples.
