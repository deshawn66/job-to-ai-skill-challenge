# Skill 06: Scorecard Explainer

## What It Does

Turns a report, dashboard, or scorecard into plain-English insight.

## Who It Helps

Leaders, managers, and sales teams who need to know what the numbers mean.

## Inputs Needed

- Report screenshot or exported table
- Metric definitions
- Time period
- Audience
- Decision the report should support

## Tools It May Interact With

- Spreadsheet
- Dashboard
- Business database
- Reporting tool

## Prompt

Explain this report for a non-technical business audience. Start with the headline, then the three most important insights, then what changed, then what action should happen next. If a number cannot be explained from the data provided, say so clearly.

## Sample Input

This monthly scorecard shows 12 people above goal, 8 near goal, and 5 below goal. The team needs to know where to focus coaching.

## Sample Output

Headline:

Most of the team is close enough to improve with targeted coaching, but 5 people need direct attention now.

Key insights:

- 12 people are already above goal.
- 8 people are within reach and should get tactical support.
- 5 people are far enough behind that waiting another month will make the gap harder to close.

Recommended action:

Prioritize coaching for the 8 near-goal people first, then create a separate plan for the 5 at-risk people.

## Safety Notes

- Do not shame individual performers.
- Do not invent reasons behind numbers.
- Do not expose private employee performance data publicly.
